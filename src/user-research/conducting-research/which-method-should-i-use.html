Which method should I use?

As well as time, cost and resource available the method you use will depend on what you want to learn.

Example research question

“What problems do people have in (a domain or subject, such as looking after aging parents)?”

Could be researched as the question:

“What do people need from a ‘x’?” For this you could use:

  • Desk research
  • Interviews
  • Surveys
  • Customer feedback
  • Speaking to call centre/customer service colleagues
  • Speaking to colleagues in the relevant domain
  • Diary study

“Can people understand and use the service we’ve built?” For this you could use:

  • In person usability testing
  • Remote usability
  • Remote, unmoderated usability testing
  • Pop up testing
  • Google analytics analysis

Example research question

“Where would people expect to find ‘x’ in our navigation structure?”

Could be researched as the question:

“How do people categorise these topics and themes?” For this you could use:

  • Treejacking
  • Card sorting
  • Usability testing (all types)

Usability testing

Watching people use the service, often by asking them to complete certain tasks or find certain information and asking them questions regarding their experiences.

Testing can be done in several ways:

  • Lab based — recruiting and then bringing in users to the research lab in Federation
  • Remote moderated — recruiting and testing with people over Skype, hangouts etc
  • Pop up testing — visit a library or coffee shop and ask people to have a go at the service
  • Remote unmoderated — using whatusersdo.com to set up a remote, unmoderated test that is issued to users via What Users Do. You’ll receive videos of the users completing the task within 24 hours

Good for

Understanding the experience of users, identifying problems and areas for improvement. Understanding the strengths and weaknesses of competitors.

Cost

Varies from the cost of incentives for pop up testing to up to £2000 for a lab session with 5 recruited participants.

Timescales

0 days for pop up through to roughly 2 weeks for a recruited lab session.

Difficulty

Lab based and remote: hard

Pop up: medium

Unmoderated: easy

Surveys

Participants are sent a (generally online) questionnaire, which asks them to answer a series of questions.

Good for

Quantifying needs answers found in qualitative research, experiences, attitudes or beliefs.

Cost

Dependent on number of respondents and recruitment method. Can be expensive.

Tools

smartsurvey.co.uk

Many tools store data outside of the UK and so are not to be used.

Timescales

It can take several days to write a survey and will then take up to 2 weeks to gather responses.

Difficulty

Very high
