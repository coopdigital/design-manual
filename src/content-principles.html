Content design principles Alpha

Our content design principles are in Alpha. That means we're working on them.

You can read our content design style guide here.

Content designers:

  • do more than write
  • always ask why
  • don’t focus on our own opinion
  • champion simplicity
  • don’t take things at face value
  • make decisions based on evidence
  • measure what’s useful
  • are open to constructive feedback
  • make content visible early on
  • don’t take things personally

We do more than write

We work with researchers, designers, and stakeholders to create services that people need — and it’s our task to decide how best to communicate information within that service.

We always ask why

Content designers ask lots of questions. It’s so we can surface the right content, at the right time, and the right place.

We don’t focus on our own opinion

The people we design for tell us how and what to write. That’s why it’s important we respect their needs.

Champion simplicity

We’ve learned that more isn’t always better and less isn’t always clearer.

We don’t take things at face value

We test our assumptions, always.

We make decisions based on evidence

This is how we avoid pushing content to people that don’t need or want it.

We measure what’s useful

We only collect data based on what we need to improve products and services.

We are open to constructive feedback

We want our colleagues to approach and challenge us. That’s how we build better products and services.

We make content visible early on

It’s an opportunity to get everyone’s feedback and make the content visible to the whole team.

We don’t take things personally

It’s just content, and that’s why we should be kind to each other — and our own work.
