Typography

Overview

Our typography is designed to allow us to communicate effectively with everyone. By being deliberate and clear in our messages we help people to achieve their goals more easily.

The font we use is Avenir Next in regular, medium and demi bold weights. This font has clear, open letterforms and open letter spacing. These characteristics help communicate the brand identity for Co-op.

To make text easy for users to read there are three key things to do with typography:

  1. range text left
  2. use sentence (upper and lower) case
  3. use white space carefully around typography

Scale of sizes

We have a typographic scale to create clear hierarchy and coherence for readers. We have a scale for larger and smaller devices, but you can add more breakpoints and sizes in the code to suit your user needs.

“Just as regular use of time provides rhythm in music, so regular use of space provides rhythm in typography, and without rhythm the listener, or the reader, becomes disorientated and lost”.

Richard Rutter - Compose to a Vertical Rhythm

All of our spacing between lines, paragraphs and headings is derived from multiples of 8px. That is known as the baseline grid. This baseline allows us to space our text, images or any other page elements with consistency.

Horizontal space

'The measure', is the number of characters in a single line of a column of text.

“Anything from 45 to 75 characters is widely regarded as a satisfactory length of line for a single-column page set in a serifed text face in a text size. The 66-character line (counting both letters and spaces) is widely regarded as ideal. For multiple column work, a better average is 40 to 50 characters”.

The Elements of Typographic Style Applied to the Web - 2.1.2 Choose a comfortable measure

Text in the recommended scale run over no more than 7 columns of our grid (on desktop). When you work with columns, do not run over more than 4 or 5 - it's more comfortable for people reading on screen as eye movement is kept to a minimum. A good tool to help you test your measure is the Google Chrome extension 45 to 75.

What is a Co-operative?

Vertical space

All of our spacing is derived from multiples of 8px. That is known as our baseline grid. This baseline allows us to space our text, images or any other page elements using these values.

“Just as regular use of time provides rhythm in music, so regular use of space provides rhythm in typography, and without rhythm the listener, or the reader, becomes disorientated and lost”.

Richard Rutter - Compose to a Vertical Rhythm

Our base font size 20px is set on a 32px 'leading' (the height of the line that the text sits on) - 8x4 = 32.

By using this system designers and developers get this for free. By overriding the values in our scale varibles in the Toolkit in your custom stylesheet you can create your own scale. However, you must have a good reason to do this and compose to your own well thought out scale.

Titles

Titles should always be title case and descriptive of the contents of the page - someone should be able to get an indication of whether the page is for them, by reading the title. Find out more about how to write titles.

Orphaned words

One word, if left on the last line of a title or paragraph, is known as an 'orphan'. Try to avoid orphaned words in your service. It can be fixed by adding a non-breaking space (&nbsp;) between the final 2 words.

Lists

Lists are an effective way of breaking up content — they make it easier for users to scan and find information. Use bullet points to break up a list of items, or a numbered list to indicate a logical sequence. Find out how to write and structure lists.

Example

The meeting agenda will be:

  • an overview of the last month
  • a presentation
  • a special announcement

Ordered list

Numbers should only for a logical sequence of things, like a series of tasks that must be done in a certain order. They do not necessarily need a lead-in line and should be complete sentences that start with a capital letter and end with a full stop.

Example

How to put your shoes on:

  1. Put on socks
  2. Put on shoes
  3. Tie laces

Quotations
