Grid

We use a grid to structure our designs. Just like the typography and vertical spacing we use, our grid allows the creation of a consistent horizontal space.

  • Viewport sizes
  • Columns and gutters

Viewport sizes

The default grid provides classes spanning 12 columns. It gives breakpoints at the following screen sizes:

  • all — 0px and up
  • extra small — 320px and up
  • small — 480px and up
  • medium — 750px and up
  • large — 960px and up
  • extra large — 1300px and up

You can override these settings by specifying your own list of screen sizes that you want to create columns for. To keep the size of the resulting stylesheet down, tailor the settings to make sure only the necessary classes are generated.

Columns and gutters

The default grid is based on 12 columns of equal width — 1 column for small and extra small viewports. The gutter of each column is a division of the 8px basline grid:

  • extra small - 8px
  • small - 8px
  • medium - 16px
  • large - 36p
  • extra large - 32px
